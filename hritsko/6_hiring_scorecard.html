Pre-Screen
Instant Disqualifiers
Any one of these = end the conversation. These aren't negotiable. Don't rationalize exceptions at this stage.
2+ jobs in the last 12 months with no explanation.
Short tenure with no clear reason = pattern, not circumstance.
Can't start within 1 week.
For Hire 1, urgency matters. The bottleneck is costing the business now.
Transportation is uncertain or conditional.
If they can't confirm how they're getting there, reliability is already in question.
Needs flexible scheduling when the role is fixed-shift.
The operation runs on a schedule. If they can't commit, the role doesn't fit.
Asks about PTO or benefits before asking about the role.
Signals priorities that don't align with an early-stage team.
Excessive phone use during the interview.
Floor work requires presence. This is a preview of on-shift behavior.
Soft Flags
Watch For — Not Automatic Disqualifiers

These don't end the interview, but they require direct follow-up.

Vague answers across the board. If every answer is "I just did what was needed" with no specifics, there's nothing to evaluate. Push for one concrete example before moving on.
Overconfident about pace without acknowledging accuracy. Speed without care is worse than slow and accurate at this stage.
Can't name a mistake they've made. Everyone makes mistakes. Someone who can't name one either doesn't reflect or doesn't tell the truth.

Usage
How to Use This Scorecard
Before the Interview

Print one copy per candidate. Complete the meta fields (name, date, interviewer) before they walk in. Do not score during the phone screen — this is for in-person only.

During the Interview

Score each criterion as you go. Use the notes column for specific phrases or examples they gave. Don't wait until after — details fade fast.

After the Interview

Tally the score and make the recommendation call within 30 minutes. File the scorecard in /Candidates/. One physical copy per candidate if printing.
